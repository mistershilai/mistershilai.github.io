Selected Projects

Antimicrobial Supply Chain Design for Botswana’s Ministry of Health
Developing and optimizing supply chain models to improve the distribution of critical antimicrobials across Botswana. Leveraging OSRM-based distance matrices and integrated economic and epidemiological data to address logistical challenges and support public health decision-making. Presented project proposal to government officials, contributing to national health policy discussions.
Biostatistics Research: Modeling Genetic Mutation Effects
Conducting population genetics research at Princeton’s Lewis-Sigler Center for Integrative Genomics. Used SLiM simulations to study fixed genetic mutation effects and chromosomal inversions under migration scenarios. Currently preparing a research paper on three-loci continent-island models, extending foundational work in evolutionary genetics.
Disaster Resilience Analysis (Research Assistant)
Supported a project examining housing disaster resilience by analyzing large-scale building permit and FEMA risk data. Contributed to regression analyses of local investments and grant flows as a research assistant.
