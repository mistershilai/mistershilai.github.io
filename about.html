Hi! I'm Elliot, a senior at Princeton University studying Operations Research and Financial Engineering with minors in Applied & Computational Mathematics, Quantitative Economics, Global Health & Health Policy, and Bioengineering. My work sits at the intersection of health systems, biostatistics, operations research, machine learning, and economics. I'm passionate about using quantitative methods to address real-world health challenges, from optimizing antimicrobial supply chains in Botswana to modeling population genetics and vaccination program effectiveness.

Beyond research, I serve as a certified EMT, have traveled across five continents, and enjoy exploring history, geography, and music. After graduation, I'll be joining UC Berkeley as a predoctoral fellow in health economics in 2026, where I hope to continue building tools and insights that improve health outcomes for underserved populations. After my gap, I hope to attend medical school and become a dual orthopedic surgeon-statistician, working on cool ML projects that can have positive impacts on patient care and global health.

Feel free to explore my research projects, check out my hobbies and travels, or get in touch!
